# Capacity Fee

**Summary**: Fixed monthly charge based on the contracted electrical capacity (amperage) for household connections in Latvia.

**Sources**: Rekins_949553846121.pdf, Rekins_949096401706.pdf, Rekins_949258599751.pdf

**Last updated**: 2026-05-09

---

The capacity fee (maksa par jaudas uzturēšanu) is a fixed component of Latvian household electricity bills. It is charged based on the maximum contracted current (amperage) of the connection.

## How it works

The customer pays a set amount per ampere (A) per month, regardless of actual consumption. Higher amperage allows more simultaneous power draw but increases the fixed cost.

## Example

In [[rekins-949553846121]], the customer had a 20 A connection at 0.34 EUR/A, resulting in a monthly capacity fee of 6.80 EUR (source: Rekins_949553846121.pdf).

## Relation to total bill

The capacity fee is part of the [[electricity-pricing-latvia|pārvades un sadales pakalpojumi]] (transmission and distribution services) section of the bill, separate from the per-kWh energy and delivery charges.

## 2025 reduction

In 2025, a temporary state-supported reduction was applied to the capacity fee. For a 20 A connection, this amounted to **–0.56 EUR/month** (source: Rekins_949096401706.pdf). The reduction ended on 1 January 2026 (source: Rekins_949258599751.pdf). See [[capacity-fee-reduction-2025]] for details.
