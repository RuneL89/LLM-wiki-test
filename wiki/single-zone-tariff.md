# Single-Zone Tariff

**Summary**: Electricity pricing model with one flat rate for all hours of the day, as opposed to multi-zone time-of-use tariffs.

**Sources**: Rekins_949553846121.pdf

**Last updated**: 2026-05-09

---

A single-zone tariff (1 laika zonā) applies a uniform price per kWh regardless of the time of day or week. This is the simplest tariff structure for household electricity in Latvia.

## Example

The [[elektrum-ekonomiskais]] plan from [[as-latvenergo]] uses a single-zone tariff. In [[rekins-949553846121]], the energy was charged at a flat 0.11800 EUR/kWh for all 144.23 kWh consumed (source: Rekins_949553846121.pdf).

## Comparison

- **Single-zone**: one price all day. Simple but may not reward off-peak usage.
- **Multi-zone**: different prices for day/night/peak hours. *Needs verification: specific multi-zone plans in Latvia are not yet documented in the wiki.*
