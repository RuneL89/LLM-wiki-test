# Gas Distribution Tariff Reform 2026

**Summary**: Regulatory changes effective 1 January 2026 that restructured natural gas distribution tariffs and excise tax rates for Latvian households.

**Sources**: Rekins_949258599751.pdf, Rekins_949329365476.pdf, Rekins_949206612654.pdf

**Last updated**: 2026-05-09

---

Starting 1 January 2026, new rules for natural gas distribution and taxation took effect in Latvia. The changes were announced on the February 2026 bill ([[rekins-949258599751]]).

## Regulatory background

According to the notice on the bill (source: Rekins_949258599751.pdf):

- State support for **AS "Sadales tīkls"** distribution tariffs ended for household connections with capacity up to **25 A**.
- New natural gas distribution system service tariffs entered into force.
- A new natural gas excise tax rate entered into force.

## Changes in practice

### Gas distribution tariffs

| Component | Pre-2026 rate | Post-2026 rate | Change |
|-----------|---------------|----------------|--------|
| Variable (per kWh) | ~0.02809 EUR/kWh | ~0.00916 EUR/kWh | Decrease |
| Fixed (monthly) | 4.62 EUR | 6.00 EUR | Increase |

Sources: Rekins_949096401706.pdf (pre), Rekins_949258599751.pdf (post).

### Gas excise tax

The excise tax rate increased:

- **Pre-2026**: 0.00380 EUR/kWh (source: Rekins_949096401706.pdf)
- **Post-2026**: 0.00595 EUR/kWh (source: Rekins_949258599751.pdf)

### Impact on total bills

Because the variable distribution fee dropped while the fixed fee and excise tax rose, the net effect depends on consumption level. For the 20 A / 2.5 m³/h connection in these bills:

- Lower gas consumption (e.g., 8 m³) sees a smaller excise impact but pays a higher fixed fee.
- Higher gas consumption (e.g., 17 m³) benefits more from the lower variable rate but pays more excise.
