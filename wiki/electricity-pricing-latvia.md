# Electricity Pricing in Latvia

**Summary**: Structure of household electricity bills in Latvia, comprising energy, delivery, and capacity components.

**Sources**: Rekins_949553846121.pdf, Rekins_949149734463_250512_163913.pdf, Rekins_949846089330.pdf, Rekins_949364875616_250808_172750.pdf, Rekins_949234523725_250906_165709.pdf, Rekins_949895009230_251008_102353.pdf

**Last updated**: 2026-05-11

---

Household electricity bills in Latvia are typically split into three main components, as seen on bills from [[as-latvenergo]].

## Components

### 1. Energy charge

The cost of the actual electricity consumed, charged per kWh. The rate depends on the chosen tariff plan. For example, the [[elektrum-ekonomiskais]] plan charged 0.12107 EUR/kWh from April to August 2025, then dropped to 0.11800 EUR/kWh from September 2025 onward (source: Rekins_949149734463_250512_163913.pdf, Rekins_949895009230_251008_102353.pdf).

### 2. Delivery fee (pārvades un sadales pakalpojumi)

A grid usage fee charged per kWh for transporting electricity to the consumer. In the example bill, this was 0.03962 EUR/kWh (source: Rekins_949553846121.pdf).

### 3. Capacity fee (jaudas uzturēšana)

A fixed charge based on the contracted electrical capacity, measured in amperes (A). In the example bill, the customer had a 20 A connection at 0.34 EUR/A, totaling 6.80 EUR (source: Rekins_949553846121.pdf). See [[capacity-fee]] for more details.

## Tariff types

- **Single-zone tariff** — one flat rate for all hours. Used by [[elektrum-ekonomiskais]].
- *Needs verification: multi-zone tariffs may exist but are not documented in current sources.*

## VAT

Electricity services are subject to [[vat-energy-latvia|21% VAT]].
